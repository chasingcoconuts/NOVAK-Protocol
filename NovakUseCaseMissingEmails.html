NOVAK Use Case: Email Archival Integrity

Securing Communication Records Against Tampering and Deletion

Scenario: A company faces litigation or regulatory scrutiny, requiring complete, unaltered email records. NOVAK cryptographically attests to the existence and content of every archived email against the official retention policy, making retrospective deletion or alteration mathematically provable.

1. Capture NIPS Data (Metadata + Content Hash)

What is captured?
- Sender/Recipient/Timestamp
- Unique Message ID
- **SHA-256 Hash of the Email Content**
- Current Archive Location

The content itself is secured indirectly by its hash.

→

2. Generate HARMONEE Receipt (Proof of Existence)

Inputs to HARMONEE:
- NOVAK Rule (e.g., "SEC_RETENTION_RULE_7Y")
- The captured NIPS Data (including content hash)
- Current Timestamp (Proof of logging)

A receipt is generated, attesting that this email existed and complied with policy.

→

3. Update REVELATION Chain (Archive Integrity)

Result:
- The current HARMONEE links into the ongoing **REVELATION** Chain.
- The chain acts as the **immutable index** of the entire archive.

If an email is deleted, the **REVELATION Chain** fails the audit check, proving a record is missing.

**Result:** NOVAK provides an **undeniable index** of the archive. If an email is missing, the break in the **REVELATION Chain** proves **non-compliance**. If an email's content is altered, the verification fails because the stored content hash (part of the NIPS) will not match the new hash (**Law of Tamper Evidence**).
