NOVAK Use Case: VA Claim Decision Integrity

Securing the Multi-Step Adjudication Process for Veterans Benefits

Scenario: A Veterans Affairs (VA) claim moves through several stages (medical review, legal review, final approval). Each step is a critical decision based on specific rules. NOVAK ensures that the final benefit decision is based on an **unbroken, auditable sequence of validated steps**, not just the final entry.

1. Initial Decision Step (Medical)

Action: Medical reviewer assigns a disability rating (0-100%).
NOVAK Rule: References specific C.F.R. disability schedule rule.
NIPS Data: Medical findings, reviewer ID, assigned rating.

Generates **HARMONEE** proof for the medical finding.

→

2. Legal Review Step

Action: Legal team reviews medical rating and determines eligibility.
NOVAK Rule: References legal statute for service connection.
NIPS Data: Eligibility status, legal findings, previous step's **REVELATION** hash.

Generates **HARMONEE** proof for the legal decision, linked to the chain.

→

3. Final Adjudication

Action: Final system approval grants benefit percentage.
NOVAK Rule: References the final approval policy.
NIPS Data: Final percentage, full decision summary, current step's **REVELATION** hash.

The final step in the **REVELATION** chain seals the decision.

**Result:** Any appeal or audit can verify the entire history—from medical rating to final percentage—against the **immutable NOVAK Rules**. It eliminates ambiguity, speeds appeals, and proves that all mandated procedures were followed precisely (**Law of Process Integrity**).
