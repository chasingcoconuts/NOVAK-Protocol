NOVAK Use Case: Aviation Collision Forensics

Ensuring Undeniable Data Integrity for Accident Investigation

Scenario: A helicopter and an airplane collide. Investigators need to determine if Air Traffic Control (ATC) systems, flight management systems (FMS), or collision avoidance systems (TCAS) correctly executed their protocols. NOVAK provides immutable proof of every relevant system's 'decision' prior to impact.

1. Capture NIPS Data (Pre-Collision)

What is captured (per system)?
- ATC System: Commands issued, detected trajectories, warnings.
- Aircraft FMS: Flight path, altitude, speed, autopilot status.
- TCAS: Warnings issued, resolution advisories (RA), response.

This is the critical event data from each system.

→

2. Generate HARMONEE Receipts (Per Event)

Inputs to HARMONEE (per system):
- NOVAK Rule (e.g., "FAA_ATC.VECTOR_RULE", "AIRCRAFT.FMS_ENGAGE")
- The captured NIPS Data (system output)
- Precise Timestamp

Each system's executed decision generates an atomic, tamper-proof receipt.

→

3. Establish REVELATION Chains

Result:
- Separate REVELATION Chain for ATC commands.
- Separate REVELATION Chain for each aircraft's FMS.
- Separate REVELATION Chain for TCAS actions.

Multiple, parallel, immutable chains of all critical actions leading to the event.

**Result:** Accident investigators have access to **mathematically undeniable proof** of every system's executed action and decision leading up to the collision. This eliminates disputes over data logs, significantly speeds up Root Cause Analysis, and prevents post-incident data manipulation.
