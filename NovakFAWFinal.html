Key Questions & Answers

Addressing NOVAK's Implementation and Scope

Q: Does NOVAK replace our existing database or transaction system?
A: **No.** NOVAK is a cryptographic **integrity layer**. It functions as a sidecar service to your existing applications, utilizing the NIPS component to pull data, generate the proof (HARMONEE), and chain the result (REVELATION). Your core business logic and database remain untouched.
Q: What is the performance impact of generating a HARMONEE receipt?
A: The cost is negligible. **SHA-256 hashing is extremely fast.** The latency added to a transaction is typically in the low single-digit milliseconds (under 5ms) on modern hardware, making the process virtually real-time and suitable for high-frequency environments.
Q: How can a third-party auditor verify the REVELATION chain without our systems?
A: They only need three things: 1) The **NOVAK Rule** file, 2) The corresponding **NIPS Data** for the transaction, and 3) The **Previous REVELATION Hash**. Using standard SHA-256 libraries (available everywhere), they can independently regenerate the HARMONEE and the final REVELATION hash. If their result matches the stored hash, the proof is mathematically certain.
Q: What stops someone from changing the NOVAK Rule file later?
A: The **Law of Tamper Evidence** ensures this is caught. The original HARMONEE receipt was generated *using the original rule*. If the rule is changed, regenerating the chain for verification will instantly produce a completely different hash, failing the audit. The rule itself must be managed immutably (e.g., via version control locked by multiple parties).
NOVAK: Mathematical integrity, zero performance impact, universal verifiability.
